Musee du Louvre - Paris Louvre Museum

Description

The Louvre Museum (French: Musée du Louvre; English: the Louvre Museum) is one of the largest and most visited art museums and a historical monument in the world. The Louvre Museum is located in the Rive Droite Seine, the First Arrondissement in Paris, France. Nearly 35,000 objects from prehistoric times to the 19th century are exhibited in an area of 60,600 square meters.

Château de Versailles - Erasmus of Paris Château de Versailles

Description

The Palace of Versailles French: Château de Versailles is a former royal residence located in Versailles, about 12 miles (19 km) west of Paris, France. The palace is owned by the French Republic and has since 1995 been managed, under the direction of the French Ministry of Culture, by the Public Establishment of the Palace, Museum and National Estate of Versailles.

Notre Dame de Paris

Description

Notre Dame de Paris is a Catholic cathedral which has become one of the tourist icons in Paris. This place has large buildings and very magnificent architecture. This cathedral was designed in 1163 by Bishop Maurice with a gothic architecture. This historic building is visited by as many as 13 million tourists every year. There are twin towers soaring high and interesting to climb to the top, because the visitors can enjoy the view from the top of the cathedral. However, this cathedral caught fire on April 15, 2019 causing very severe damage. We're only here to remember the fire incident on April 15, 2019

Arc de Triomphe

Description

The Arc de Triomphe de l'Étoile is one of the most famous monuments in Paris built between 1806 and 1836. It is located in the center of the Place Charles de Gaulle at the western end of the Champs-Élysées. Although there have been many modifications of the original building design, reflecting political changes and power struggles, this motif still retains the strong essence of the original concept. The overall decorative style of the arch with the sculptural tradition of the first half of the nineteenth century makes it quite extraordinary. Used as a tribute to those who fought for France, in particular, who fought during the Napoleonic Wars. Engraved in and above the arch, were all the names of the Generals who defended the war. There are inscriptions in the ground beneath the arched vault that cover the tomb of an unknown soldier from World War I.

Champs-Élysées

Description

For those of you tourists who want to shop, take a walk, or just to take pictures while enjoying the atmosphere of Paris, you can choose this famous tourist destination. The Champs-Élysées is a street about 1.9 km long and 70 meters wide, which serves as a link between Place del la Concorde and Place Charles de Gaulle (site of the Arc de Triomphe). Then, visitors can enjoy various cafes and can shop from well-known branded shops. The streets here are very busy and crowded with visitors from among young people as well as tourists from various countries. While traveling, tourists can see ancient buildings or buildings, some monuments, and amazing statues.

Sacré-Cœur

Description

Situated atop the hill from Montmartre, this exotic church is one of the best building views in Paris. If you are a fan of the architectural world, then you will consider art and a place of worship combined. Sacre-Coeur is visited by more than 10 million visitors annually, while Notre Dame is visited by around 13 million visitors annually. In addition, this basilica has a very special architectural style. You can visit the basilica, you can enter for free and you can buy a ticket to enter the Dome to see the sights of Paris.

Tuileries Garden

Description

Tuileries Garden is a public garden located between the Louvre and Place de la Concorde. Then this park becomes a great place for a walk or just a break in Paris. Visitors will find flowers blooming beautifully. In addition, you can relax on the grass while enjoying food for a while and enjoy the quiet pool.

Jardin du Luxembourg

Description

The Jardin du Luxembourg otherwise known as the Luxembourg Gardens located in Paris, was originally created in 1612 by Marie de' Medici, widow of King Henry IV of France. This beautiful garden has an area of 25 hectares of land divided into French gardens and English gardens. Between the two lies a geometric forest and a large pond. Then, there is an old orchard with various apple orchards. There is an apiary, especially for learning about beekeeping and a greenhouse with a collection of orchids and a rose garden. The gardens here have 106 statues scattered throughout the park, the monumental Medici fountain, Orangerie and Pavillon Davioud. Apart from that, there are many activities and facilities for children to play in the park.

Montmartre

Description

Montmartre is a large and famous hill which is at an altitude of 130 meters. At the top of this hill is a historic site, the Basilica of Sacré-Cœur. Montmartre is the best place to enjoy the magnificent view of Paris from above. In this tourist area there are many shops, street artists, souvenir sellers, and cafes.

Musée d'Orsay

Description

that's not right if your vacation to Paris doesn't take the time to visit the famous Musée d'Orsay. The Musée d'Orsay Museum used to be a train station, but now has a great collection of art. The tourists not only find paintings, but there are statues and furniture as a collection of this museum. There are many paintings by Monet, Degas, Renoir, and other famous artists. Then, the architecture of the Musée d'Orsay building is amazing, when visitors come out they will get views of the Seine River and the Sacre Coeur from afar. In addition, the museum offers a restaurant downstairs for those who want to enjoy a meal.

Description

For those of you who travel to Paris with children and family, you can choose Disneyland Paris attractions. Place de la Concorde

Description

Place de la Concorde is a popular square as a tourist attraction when on vacation to Paris. This famous square is octagonal in shape bounded by a moat. Visitors will find an object in the form of an Egyptian Obelisk with a height of 23 meters in the middle of the Place de la Concorde. Then, you can find works of art in the form of very extraordinary statues to be immortalized. In addition, visitors will find two fountains that adorn part of the Place de la Concorde, namely La fontaine des Mers and the Elevation of the Maritime.

Le Marais

Description

Le Marais is a very famous historical district and old city in Paris, France. This area is filled with various types of shops, food and others. It has many outstanding historical buildings and very remarkable architecture. Le Marais is the closest to getting a medieval Parisian feel and has more buildings and streets left intact than anywhere else in Paris. At a glance some of the beautiful buildings and houses show the status of the residents. After the French Revolution, most of the area was abandoned by rich and poor, so the area is considered so dirty today it is almost destroyed by city officials who want to modernize Paris.

Description

For those of you tourists who want to enjoy the beauty of the Eiffel tower while enjoying the green area, you can choose the Champ de Mars as a tourist destination. Champ de Mars is a green open space for visitors who want to relax and have fun with friends and children. Palais Garnier

Description

Palais Garnier, also known as the Opera National de Paris, is a famous opera theater in Paris. This magnificent building was designed by Baron Haussmann in 1858 and built by Charles Garnier in 1861. This tourist attraction is very popular with its very beautiful interior and can accommodate an audience of up to a capacity of about 2000 people. The design of this building is very unique, there is a large chandelier that weighs up to 7 tons. Then there are stairs made of white marble from Italy, green marble from Sweden, and beautiful red marble. Many other places are also inspired by this magnificent building.

Montparnasse Tower

Description

For those of you tourists who want to enjoy the beautiful view of Paris from a height, you can choose Montparnasse Tower as a place of observation. Montparnasse Tower is a skyscraper with a height of 210 meters and was built from 1969 to 1972. Visitors can enjoy 360° views that are incredible at night and during the day. Like the London Eye in England, the observation deck of the Montparnasse Tower will provide a unique view of Paris so that it will provide an unforgettable experience for you.

Panthéon

Description

The Panthéon is a historic building in Paris with typical European architecture that adorns the facade. This magnificent building is said to be the grave of several important French figures such as Louis Braille, Marie and Pierre Curie, Victor Hugo, and many more. This Panthéon tour when viewed from the outside has a very magnificent architectural style and is very good for taking pictures when on vacation to Paris.

Rodin Museum

Description

The Musée Rodin otherwise known as the Rodin Museum is a famous museum which opened in 1919. This museum is dedicated to the works of the French sculptor Auguste Rodin. Works with a collection by Mrs muse Camille Claudel are also on display. Stroll through the vast gardens and discover some of the master's masterpieces. So it's not fun if you don't stop by the Rodin museum to better understand the history.

Conciergerie

Description

The Conciergerie is a building in Paris that was previously a prison, but is currently used as a court of law. This tour provides examples of outstanding architecture from the rest of the 14th century. Here visitors will find the crypt as well as the Chapel where Marie Antoinette was held during the French Revolution and which is now dedicated to her. This attraction is open daily from 09.30am to 6pm and is closed on certain special days. Admission is 9€ for adults, 7€ for EU citizens (18-25 y.o.), and free for under 18s.

Pont des Arts

Description

The Pont des Arts or Passerelle des Arts is a pedestrian bridge in Paris that crosses the River Seine. This bridge connects the Institut de France and the Palais du Louvre square. Between 1802 and 1804, during the reign of Napoleon I, nine arches of metal pedestrian bridges were built on the site of the present Pont des Arts. The Pont des Arts was the first metal bridge in Paris. Engineers Louis-Alexandre de Cessart and Jacques Dillon were originally going to create a bridge that would resemble a suspended garden, with trees, flowerbeds, and benches. So that it will add to the impression of a beautiful bridge when on vacation to Paris.

Musée de l'Orangerie

Description

For those of you who like popular works of art or just taking pictures while on vacation to Paris, you can choose this museum as a tourist destination. The Musée de l'Orangerie or known as the Museum I'Orangerie has a collection of paintings and sculptures by famous artists. A large collection of great artists including Claude Monet, Paul Cézanne, Henri Matisse, Amedeo Modigliani, Pablo Picasso, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Henri Rousseau, Alfred Sisley, Chaim Soutine, Maurice Utrillo, and many more.

Grand Palais

Description

The Grand Palais is a large historical site, exhibition hall and museum located on the Champs-Élysées, Paris, France. Construction of the Grand Palais began in 1897 following the demolition of the Palais Industrie as part of the preparation of works for the 1900, Universal exhibition, which also included the establishment of the adjacent Petit Palais and Pont Alexandre III. The structure is built in the Beaux-Arts architectural style taught by the cole des Beaux-Arts Paris. The building reflects the sense of movement for ornate decoration through stonework, the formality of floor planning and the use of innovative techniques at the time, such as with glass domes, structures made of iron and steel framing light, and the use of reinforced ones.

La Villette

Description

La Villette is a large and famous park with an area of 55.5 hectares. The park is home to several important places in Paris including the Cité des Sciences et de l'Industrie (Museum City of Science and Industry), concert venues, and the Conservatoire de Paris. Visitors who vacation to La Villette can enjoy museums, theaters, architectural buildings, beautiful gardens, and open spaces. In addition, La Villette provides a playground area that can be used for both children and adults.
